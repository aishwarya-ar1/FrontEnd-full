About

Hey, There!
We present to you the tiniest treasure to win the battle against COVID and in the future to gain victory over anything that may affect to your well-being. Health kit is a website that helps to understand and predict the severity of covid in patients with underlying and chronic diseases so that they could receive accurate treatments and significantly diminish long-term side effects. Covid heath kit incorporates IOT covid screening, this helps to practice no contact covid screening this helps to control the infection even further, predicting if the patient is affected with covid using CT scan imaging.

With infections spreading at a rapid rate, it is important to know and keep up with preventable diseases, health kit also incorporates a vaccination page to keep up with vaccinations and know your options in vaccines available for your age as recommended by the NHS and CDC.

Our project also supports a food delivery system where you can order healthy, freshly prepared food and groceries that can be delivered by practicing the no-contact policy.

As COVID-19 has overwhelmed health systems worldwide, debates around resilience have become more urgent, and there is a need to better understand the elements of national responses through a resilience lens. Thus, in this perspective, we use machine learning and IoT to predict the severity of patients who are vulnerable to support our health care system so that they can make an accurate diagnosis at ease.

The key benefits of the health kit are remarkable. The spread of COVID-19 in India depends on a lot of factors such as religious congregation, social contact structure, low testing rates, identification of COVID-19 suspects, measures such as lockdown and sealing of hot stop, etc. taken by the Indian government.
Adding to this in the era where technology and medicine combined made significant improvements in the healthcare system, it's important to incorporate machine learning models for accurate prognosis, especially during a worldwide pandemic where such models can save many lives
